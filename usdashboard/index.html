Key Market Insights

Product Gaps to Address

SMS at scale — High-volume outbound SMS (50K+/month) with AI qualification and live rep handoff is a validated US use case. Platform needs to prove it can handle this volume reliably. No voice capability is an additional trade-off for SMS-heavy prospects.

Multi-tenancy came up in 3 of 7 calls. Agencies and partners can't manage multiple clients efficiently. #1 product gap for the US agency/partner segment.

No voice capability — validated as US roadmap priority. Not a dealbreaker now but will be for future US customers.

No local 10DLC numbers — toll-free only. Fine for national firms but problematic for local businesses (validated again by Kayalar Motors).

No email channel — second call (Hongfa + Kayalar) where email is the dealbreaker. Auto dealers receive 90%+ of leads via email from listing sites. Manufacturing needs outbound email. Growing blocker theme (not critical for STANBERT — WhatsApp-first).

Toll-free / number porting ambiguity — STANBERT wants same toll-free number for both WhatsApp and SMS. Can't confirm this works in SleekFlow today. Number portability and shared registration need clear documentation.

Salesforce conversation history sync — Full WhatsApp conversation history doesn't sync natively to Salesforce. Native integration handles contacts/leads/opps but NOT conversation content. Workaround exists (Zapier link or API), but this will be a recurring ask from Salesforce-heavy prospects.

US WhatsApp marketing broadcast uncertainty — Meta restricts marketing templates for US numbers. US prospects want outbound WhatsApp marketing but regulatory clarity is needed. This limits use cases like lead nurture and re-engagement campaigns for mainland US numbers.

Vertical-specific SaaS integrations — Prospects in healthcare, dental, auto, and other verticals expect integration with their industry-specific software (e.g. Dentrix, DealerSocket). These are niche but recurring asks that could unlock entire verticals if supported.

Strongest Market Signals

High-volume SMS + AI qualification — People's Tax Relief validates SMS as THE primary US channel. POC needed to confirm capabilities at scale.

Dental / Healthcare in US territories & Caribbean — WhatsApp-heavy, appointment-driven, FAQ-friendly for AI bots. SmilesInc (Bermuda) and EvokeDM both validate the dental vertical. Caribbean and US territory markets use WhatsApp as the default channel — broadcast restrictions don't apply like they do for mainland US numbers. Dentrix integration could unlock the dental vertical further if API is available.

International WhatsApp for US B2B — US companies discovering WhatsApp at global conferences (PerfAI). Repeatable.

Agency multiplier deals — one agency = 20-100+ end clients. Worth solving multi-tenancy for.

Respond.io competitor displacement — STANBERT LLC (Closed Won) left Respond.io due to zero human support. Validates that superior onboarding + US-based support is a real competitive differentiator. Trucking/logistics = WhatsApp-heavy vertical.

US-based businesses with LATAM sales teams — US and Canadian companies with LATAM go-to-market teams need WhatsApp + CRM integration. Salesforce integration is a key differentiator in vendor evaluations. Repeatable pattern across any US-based company selling into Latin America.

Legal / WhatsApp banning pain (US territories & Caribbean) — DMVV Law (DR) getting constantly banned on WhatsApp Business due to high volume. WhatsApp Business API via SleekFlow directly solves this. Meta ads → WhatsApp funnel, document collection via chat. Repeatable across legal firms in US territories, Caribbean, and Canada where WhatsApp is the default client channel.

US Hispanic market = WhatsApp-first — Clean Credit Consulting (Orlando) and DMVV Law both confirm: Hispanic-serving US businesses use WhatsApp as their primary customer channel. Radio ads → WhatsApp, Meta ads → WhatsApp. Credit repair, legal, tax — any Hispanic-market vertical in FL, TX, CA is a natural prospect.
